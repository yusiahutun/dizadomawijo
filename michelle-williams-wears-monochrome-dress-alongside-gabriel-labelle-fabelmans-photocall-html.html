Michelle Williams wears monochrome dress alongsideGabriel LaBelle atThe Fabelmans photocall

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Tuesday, September 17, 2024

Michelle Williams looked flawless at a photocall for The Fabelmans in London on Thursday as she joined co-star Gabriel LaBelle.

She wore a black mini dress which had a white collar as she posed for photographs alongside Gabriel who wore a grey jacket and black skinny jeans. 

The photocall comes as the movie led the BAFTA snubs that day, despite being widely nominated in the  US during award season.

The director's alter ego in the film is Sammy Fabelman, the firstborn child and only son of Burt and Mitzi, who, in turn, are thinly disguised versions of Spielberg's own parents: Arnold Spielberg and his wife Leah.

And while The Fabelmans won Best Motion Picture Drama and Best Director — Motion Picture at the Golden Globes, and appeared in five BAFTA longlist categories, it only received one BAFTA nomination for best original screenplay. 

Likewise, Top Gun: Maverick‘s scored top nominations at the Golden Globes, but it was shut out of the Best Film, Director, Actor, and Screenplay nominations.

Despite all this, The Fabelmans is 'the bravest film' Steven Spielberg has ever made, the movie's producer has said.

Kristie Macosko Krieger said the semi-autobiographical film was a story that the Oscar-winning director was always going to tell, though he had not 'consciously' known it.

Interesting: The Fabelmans is a semi-autobiographical film. It details the dissolution of the marriage of Mitzi (Michelle Williams) and Burt Fabelman (Paul Dano) as seen through the eyes of Steven Spielberg's surrogate, played by Gabriel, (right) 

The pair have worked together for 26 years, on films including Ready Player One, West Side Story and Indiana Jones And The Kingdom Of The Crystal Skull.

Speaking at the film's London premiere, Macosko Krieger said: 'It's a movie that's been a long time in the making.

'I think Steven was gonna make this movie for his entire life, but he actually didn't know it.

'I've worked with him for 26 years to actually be a part of this specific movie which honours his mother and father and his family in a really special way - people that I knew and people that I love - it's really special.'

Asked about whether Spielberg was 'destined' to make the film, she continued: 'I don't think he consciously knew it, but I think subconsciously this is a story that has been in him since his family experienced what they experienced when he was a young boy.

'I think the bravest film he's ever made. I think he's a very personal human but this is like putting your heart out on the table and letting people look at and examine it.

'Hopefully people feel really good about what he's done.'

The Fabelmans picked up a Screen Actors Guild award nomination for outstanding performance by a cast in a motion picture.

Spielberg also continued his award season rivalry with Martin McDonagh, after both were nominated for outstanding directorial achievement in a theatrical feature film for 2022 by the Directors Guild of America (DGA).

The film is also hotly tipped to pick up nods at the Oscar nominations, which are due to be announced next week on January 24.

The BAFTAs will be broadcast on BBC One and BBC iPlayer on February 19.

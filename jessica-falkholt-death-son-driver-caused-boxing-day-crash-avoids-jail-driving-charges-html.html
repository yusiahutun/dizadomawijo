Jessica Falkholt death: Son of driver who caused Boxing Day crash avoids jail on driving charges

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Wednesday, April 3, 2024

The son of a serial driving offender who caused the car crash that killed a Home and Away actress and her family has been given a jail sentence for dangerous driving.

Shane Andrew Whitall was ordered to serve an intensive correction order (ICO) and attend a rehab centre after being found guilty of reckless and dangerous driving.

It was Whitall's father Craig who caused the crash that killed actress Jessica Falkholt, 28, her dad Lars, 69, mother Vivian, 60, and sister Annabelle, 21, on Boxing Day 2017. 

Shane Whitall, 28, was initially received a good behaviour bond, but after breaching his order returned to Nowra Local Court last week where he was sentenced to a 12-month ICO.

Police caught him driving a Toyota Camry dangerously and unlicensed in September 2017, at which time he was on his learner's permit.

Shane Andrew Whitall (left) has narrowly avoided jail on dangerous driving charges. His father Craig (right) was a serial driving offender who caused the crash that killed the family of Home and Away actress Jessica Falkholt on Boxing Day 2017

Home and Away star Jessica Falkholt (far right), 28, her father Lars (far left), 69, mother Vivian (left), 60, and sister Annabelle (right), 21, were all killed in the horror crash south of Sydney

He caught the eye of police after failing to obey a stop sign, but initially pleaded not guilty in court. 

The charges against him were found to be proven at a hearing, as were a number of other offences including drug cultivation and break an enter.

Craig Whitall veered across the centre line of the Princes Highway and crashed head on into the Falkholt family's car.

A serial driving offender, he had more than 200 convictions - including many driving offences - and had spent time in prison.

Despite his criminal record, his son Shane claimed he still viewed him as his 'idol'.

'He was an awesome father, the best. I couldn't ask for anything more of him,' Shane told Seven News in the days after the horror crash.

'He did what he could and that's all you can ask of anyone. He was a hero in my eyes, he was my idol.' 

The Falkholt family were travelling home from a Christmas lunch at Ulladulla, on the New South Wales south coast, on December 26, 2017, when the crash occurred.

Whitall - a heroin addict - was driving home to Ulladulla from a methadone clinic an hour away in Nowra, when he crossed onto the wrong side of the road.  

The force of the crash caused both cars to burst into flames.

Other motorists risked their lives to pull Whitall and the Falkholt's from the wreckage.

Whitall was killed instantly, while Lars and Vivian Falkholt - who were in the front seat of the car - also died at the scene.

The fiery crash on the Princes Highway on December 26, 2017, killed Lars and Vivian Falkholt instantly and left their two daughters fighting for life in hospital. Annabelle died three days later, while Jessica's life support was turned off on January 17, 2017

Craig Whitall was a heroin addict who was driving home from a methadone clinic when his car crossed onto the wrong side of the road, hitting the Falkholt family's vehicle head-on

Jessica - a rising star of the Australian film and television industry - was just weeks away from making her big screen debut in locally-made movie 'Harmony'

Jessica Falkholt's life support was turned off a day after the funeral service for her parents and sister

Annabelle and Jessica were rushed to hospital and placed into comas, both suffering serious brain injuries.

The younger sister died three days after the crash, while Jessica's underwent surgery but her life support was eventually turned off on January 17, 2018.

Her death came just a day after the funeral service for her parents and sister.

Jessica - a rising star of the Australian film and television industry - was farewelled in a moving service a week later.

Her Home and Away co-stars including Pia Miller, Olivia Deeble, Isabella Giovinazzo and Jackson Heywood were in attendance at the funeral.

Jessica's uncle Paul Ponticello choked back tears as he condemned the drug-addict driver who caused the crash.

'An entire family has been taken by events totally beyond their control,' Mr Ponticello told the hundreds of mourners inside the church.

'What occurred does not fit neatly within the definition of accident.

'A heartfelt thanks goes out to those who risked their safety to drag both Jessica and Annabelle from the wreckage, which gave the girls, albeit slim, a chance of survival.'

Major upgrades have been made to the stretch of road south of Sydney in the wake of the horror crash that killed the Falkholt family.

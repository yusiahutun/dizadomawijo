The 6 Best Alternatives to MyFitnessPal for Android and iPhone

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Thursday, February 1, 2024

When it comes to apps that help you track your diet and exercise, MyFitnessPal is a leading name. The MyFitnessPal app was first launched in 2005, growing a large fanbase before being sold to Under Armour in 2015. In October 2020, the MyFitnessPal app changed hands once more and is now owned by the private equity firm Francisco Partners.

Although MyFitnessPal remains one of the most popular health and fitness mobile apps, it now has an army of competition. Here are some of the best mobile app alternatives to MyFitnessPal.

1. Lifesum

3 Images Close

One of the most popular diet and exercise tracking alternatives to MyFitnessPal is Lifesum. The app has tried to move away from labels such as “calorie counter” and “diet app” instead labeling itself a “digital self-care app.”

Lifesum’s interface is colorful and quirky, making it a hit with fitness influencers and popular with a modern audience. In just a few clicks, you can create your custom profile, set dietary goals, choose dietary preferences, and get started on a hassle-free meal plan.

The app has a vast food database featuring millions of food items, plus a built-in barcode scanner to make updating your food diary a little bit easier. If you’re trying to drink more water or up your fruit and vegetable intake, then Lifesum’s habit trackers can help you build lasting, healthy habits.

The basic version of Lifesum is free to use, but you will need to pay a small monthly subscription to access its full features.

Download: Lifesum for Android | iOS (Free, subscription available)

2. Lose It!

3 Images Close

Lose It! Is an app entirely dedicated to helping its users lose weight. It has one of the largest food tracking databases available—containing more than 34 million foods—making it as easy as possible to keep track of everything you eat.

While setting up your profile, you’ll be prompted to answer a variety of lifestyle questions, including if you’ve tried to lose weight in the past and what motivates you to reach your goals.

It’s completely free to set up an account and start tracking your diet, but you will need to upgrade if you’re looking to unlock extra features such as custom goals and habit tracking.

Download: Lose It! for Android | iOS (Free, subscription available)

3. Calorie Counter by FatSecret

2 Images Close

The Calorie Counter App by FatSecret can be used independently or in sync with FatSecret Professional to share your food and exercise data with a healthcare professional. Its easy-to-use food diary is designed to help you make food tracking a habit, and it even has an image recognition feature that will take a picture of your meal and analyze its nutritional content.

If you want to keep a food diary, you can store these food images in your own food photo album, allowing you to keep a photo diet of food snaps.

Although Calorie Counter is primarily marketed as a weight loss app, it also helps people maintain their current weight or gain weight, if that's your goal.

Download: Calorie Counter by FatSecret for Android | iOS (Free, subscription available)

4. Cronometer

3 Images Close

Cronometer is one of the most detailed food tracking apps available. It allows you to track up to 82 micronutrients, and even its free version provides more nutritional information than most of its competitors.

The interface is modern, colorful, and uncluttered considering the amount of data the app displays. Dark mode fans will be happy to hear there is a dark theme available. The app aims to support a variety of different diets, including intermittent fasting with its built-in fasting timer, and keto with its keto calculator.

The app has an extensive library of exercises, including regular household activities, and even estimates how many calories you burn when driving your vehicle or as the pilot of a plane! For access to premium features such as timestamps, the fasting timer, and nutrient oracle, you will need to upgrade to Cronometer Gold.

Download: Cronometer for Android | iOS (Free, subscription available)

5. Open Food Facts

3 Images Close

Open Food Facts is a non-profit app designed to help its users make informed food choices by helping them to decipher food labels. Using it, you can either search or scan the label of a particular product to see its nutritional score, carbon footprint, ingredient list, and allergens.

Unlike other locked databases, any user can update a product on Open Food Facts, ensuring that product data stays up to date. If you’re looking for a calorie counter, then this app isn’t for you. But if you’re trying to educate yourself or simplify your grocery shopping, then it can help you navigate confusing labels.

Download: Open Food Facts for Android| iOS (Free)

6. Calorie Counter +

3 Images Close

The Calorie Counter + app by Nutracheck is part of the service's online food diary service. The app is intuitive and easy to use; few taps are needed to add an item to your food diary. To make tracking even more simple, each food item has an accompanying image or logo to help you recognize the right product.

Along with tracking calories, the app also allows you to record your exercise and can even be configured to track your walks with Strava. There’s the option to set daily targets for water, fruit, and veggies too. Although there is a free Lite membership available, the app works best when upgraded to a premium subscription.

If you want to try before you buy, Nutracheck offers a free seven-day trial of its full service.

Download: Calorie Counter +  for Android | iOS (Free, subscription available)

Finding the Best Alternatives to MyFitnessPal

Whatever your reason for choosing an alternative to MyFitnessPal, it’s reassuring to know that there are plenty of other health and wellness app options. Whether you’re trying to lose weight, maintain your weight, or gain weight, food tracking apps are a useful tool to have in your pocket when used correctly.

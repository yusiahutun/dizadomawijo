10 best dog-friendly B&amp;Bs in the UK for 2023

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, October 3, 2024
1

Burnhopeside Hall, Durham

Booking

Nestled in the middle of a 475-acre estate near Durham, this dog-friendly B&B is a listed Georgian house decked out with elegant furnishings, sofas you can sink into and roaring log fires at every turn. At Burnhopeside Hall, there are even great sash windows looking out over the woodlands for you to channel your inner-Pride and Prejudice.

The resident springer spaniels are welcoming to all pooches at the dog-friendly B&B, so bring your four-legged friend and stroll the magnificent lawns or cycle alongside the river. Oversized beds, luxurious linen and fresh flowers also await.

2

Forthay Bed and Breakfast, Gloucestershire

Forthay Bed and Breakfast/Booking

Among the wooden beams and wonky floors, this characterful farmhouse is filled with some of the owners' (Deborah and Charles) favourite things: books, fresh flowers and enchanting artwork to name a few.

At dog-friendly B&B Forthay, your pooch will enjoy racing around in the garden, once they've enjoyed their complimentary treats, of course. They'll also be welcomed by very sociable cockapoos, Monte, Margot and Molly. Meanwhile, you can curl up by the log-burner and toast marshmallows, play croquet on the lawn or head out to the Cotswolds (just 15 minutes away) for an idyllic country ramble.

BOOK NOW

3

Zeal Farm, Somerset

Booking.com

Zeal Farm looks like a house out of a story book. Set in Dulverton in Somerset, the owners at this dog-friendly B&B offer guests (and their dogs) the opportunity to immerse themselves in nature, and the charismatic charm that this region is famed for.

You can enjoy a continental or à la carte breakfast each day, before heading out on one of the nearby hikes or exploring the gardens with your four-legged friend.

4

Penbontbren Luxury Bed and Breakfast﻿, Ceredigion

Booking.com

The Penbontbren Luxury Bed and Breakfast, sitting in 32 acres of its own idyllic slice of countryside, in a converted 1860s farmhouse is only two miles from relatively unknown beaches.

Its rooms are elegant - with painted beams and adjoining sitting areas - and owners Huw and Richard encourage you to bring your own wine or to buy provisions from the farm shop. The friendly pair are also on hand to offer pet-friendly walking and activity advice.

BOOK NOW

5

Stow House, North Yorkshire

Booking

Transforming a former vicarage into a quirky, dog-friendly B&B, the owners of Stow House in the Yorkshire Dales have left no corner untouched in their quest to create a haven for dog lovers.

Views across Wensleydale, Bishopdale and acres of private garden, frame the Victorian interiors with a gorgeous green hue. Floors, banisters and sash windows have been restored, and sofas given new life with velvet covering. Expect cocktails expertly created by owner Sarah, and hearty breakfasts served up by her husband, Phil.

BOOK NOW

6

Belmount Hall, Cumbria

Belmount Hall

Formerly owned by none other than Beatrix Potter, the illustrious Belmount Hall sits proudly in the heart of the Lake District, offering views across Esthwaite Water and the Lakeland Hills.

The dog-friendly B&B's bedrooms have had their period features accentuated, and complemented by both antique pieces and modern en-suites (complete with a power shower).

Dog-friendly pub grub can be found in the centre of Hawkshead, just a 15-minute walk away, while the shores of Windermere is just under 20 minutes away by car.

BOOK NOW

7

Willow House Bed & Breakfast, Londonderry

Booking.com

With an almost perfect rating from previous guests, Willow House Bed & Breakfast embraces families and pets in all their chaos. Just a few miles from the Roe Valley Country Park, where cycling and hiking are a must, the dog-friendly B&B sets its guests up for the day with a choice of an à la carte or a full English or Irish breakfast.

Larger family rooms are available and the guest house's garden is available for everyone to use, plus the terrace is a lovely place to relax on a sunny day.

BOOK NOW

8

St Michael's Bistro, Gloucestershire

Booking.com

Tucked away in the wilds just north of Stroud, Painswick is a perfect chocolate-box-pretty village known as 'Queen of the Cotswolds'. A perfect base for scampering along the Cotswold Way with the dog, St Michael's Bistro is an intimate guesthouse set in a beautiful 17th-century cottage.

It offers three dog-friendly rooms and two larger suites, which cater well for families. Our favourite is the Bohemian Suite, which has a gorgeous wooden four-poster bed inlaid with stained glass, plus a freestanding copper tub. And you don't have to go very far for a fine feast. Downstairs is the bistro, a cosy little spot with exposed beams and flagstone floors, which offers a topnotch array of modern British and European dishes.

9

Victorian House, Cumbria

Booking.com

Moments from the cutesy shops and tea rooms of Grasmere village, this family and dog-friendly B&B makes an ideal base for snuffling around the forests and fells of the Lake District. Housed in a sprawling ivy-covered Victorian house, the communal areas are furnished with beautiful vintage furniture and quirky objet d'art.

Victorian House has some dog-friendly rooms for travellers bringing their pooch. These have hard flooring and cosy furniture. There is also a warm water tap outside to wash off muddy paws after days out exploring.

10

Parkhead House, West Lothian

Booking.com

Set near many Outlander locations, this dog-friendly B&B is ideal for TV fans in search of a Scottish staycation. The town of Queensferry is just west of Edinburgh and close to Forth Bridge. At Parkhead House, there is a garden with direct access to open countryside.

A former gamekeeper's cottage, the B&B is over 300 years old, yet the interiors are modern and cosy. A Scottish breakfast is served each morning and up to two dogs are welcome, with plenty of woodland walks and space to throw a ball around.

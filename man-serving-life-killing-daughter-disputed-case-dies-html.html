Father serving life for killing girl in disputed case dies

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, August 23, 2024

A South Carolina prison inmate who vehemently maintained that DNA evidence cleared him of raping and killing his 12-year-old daughter has died while serving a life sentence in prison.

Billy Wayne Cope, 53, passed away in state custody on Thursday, apparently of natural causes, Richland County Coroner Gary Watts said. An autopsy has been ordered by law.

Days after the November 29, 2001, killing of Amanda Cope, her father confessed three times to investigators. 

Prosecutors charged him with murder and rape. But when DNA tests came back, the only genetic material on the girl's body was from a neighbor down the street.

Inmate's death: Billy Wayne Cope, 53, died on Thursday while serving a life sentence for the 2001 rape and murder of his 12-year-old daughter, Amanda Cope, after vehemently denying for years that he committed the crimes 

Amanda was found dead inside her family's home in Rock Hill, South Carolina, in 2001

Prosecutors then said Cope allowed James Sanders into his home knowing he was going to attack his daughter, and that Cope had attacked the girl previously. 

Both Cope and Sanders were convicted of murder and sentenced to life without parole.

Accomplice: Cope's neighbor, James Sanders, was also convicted of rape and murder and sentenced to life in prison 

Cope's attorneys said prosecutors were just molding the evidence to send an innocent man to prison and Cope had mental problems and was so devastated by his daughter's death that investigators were able to manipulate him into confessing to a crime he did not commit.

But the state Supreme Court upheld Cope's conviction in 2013 and the US Supreme Court refused to hear his case.

Cope's attorneys said that their inability to get Cope, a married father-of-three, out of prison was one of the most disappointing parts of their careers.

Prosecutors said the Cope home in Rock Hill was locked and secure when 12-year-old Amanda was found strangled to death, so someone had to let Sanders in.

Defense attorneys countered that the home's lock could be easily picked with a credit card or driver's license.

The home was also filled with buckets of human waste, trash and other filth, making it impossible for a stranger to navigate the mess in the dark, said prosecutors, who also presented evidence Cope had sexually assaulted his daughter before the night she was killed.

'Billy Wayne Cope was a cruel and selfish individual who elevated his own sick desires above the welfare, happiness and ultimately the life of his own daughter. Today I only feel sorrow for his two surviving children,' prosecutor Kevin Brackett told The Herald after learning Cope had died.

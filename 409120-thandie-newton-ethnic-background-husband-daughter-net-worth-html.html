Thandie Newton: Ethnic background, husband, daughter, net worth

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Tuesday, May 14, 2024

Thandie Newton is one of the top actresses in the world. She has starred in several films such as Mission Impossible 2, Crash, For Coloured Girls, and Beloved.

Who is Thandie Newton – the woman, not just the actress? While she is a household name in the entertainment industry, there is more to her than just acting. Here is more about her childhood to her ethnicity, royalty roots, family, career, and net worth.

Thandie Newton profile summary

  • Name: Melanie Thandiwe Newton
  • Date of birth: November 6, 1972 (48 years)
  • Place of birth: London, United Kingdom
  • Education: Downing College, Cambridge
  • Profession: Actress
  • Husband: Oliver Ol Parker (married 1998 - present)

Early life

Thandie Newton (real Melanie Thandiwe Newton) was born on November 6, 1972, in London, United Kingdom. There had been rumours that her birthplace is in Zambia. However, this is not true; her parents lived and worked in Zambia but visited London.

Thandie Newton is of Zimbabwean and British ancestry. Her mother is of Shona ethnicity - one of the largest ethnic tribes in Zimbabwe – while her father is of British descent.

Thandie spent three years as a child in Zambia, where her parents worked in the healthcare sector. However, her parents would later move back to England – with her father taking up the family business. She then spent the rest of her childhood in Penzance, Cornwall.

Thandie Newton attended St. Mary’s Roman Catholic school and later a private school – The School of Claire – in Penzance. She has admitted to feeling out of place while growing up, especially while attending catholic school. To start with, she was a black girl with African roots attending an all-white school. She was also an atheist in a Catholic school operated by nuns.

Growing up in Penzance was not easy for Thandie, despite the beautiful environment. She was a victim of racial abuse by bullies – the town was primarily white in racial composition. She also faced a lot of pressure to fit in. For example, her name Thandiwe – an African name meaning 'beloved' - was 'whitewashed' to a more western version – Thandie. It is only recently that she has reclaimed her real name.

While growing up, Thandiwe attended Tring School for the Performing Arts, where she studied dance. Later, after finishing her secondary education, she enrolled at Downing College in Cambridge. In 1995, she graduated with a Social Anthropology degree from the institution.

Career

Thandiwe started her career in the film industry in 1991, with her debut role in the movie Flirting. She would later land another role in 1994, acting alongside Tom Cruise and Brad Pitt in the film Interview with the Vampire. However, her breakthrough came in 1995 when she played the role of Sally Hemings in Jefferson’s film in Paris.

Following her Jefferson in Paris role, Thandie was cast in the filmed Beloved as a titular character. She played alongside other top stars – Danny Glover and Oprah Winfrey. Later, she would land another role in Mission Impossible 2 and several other films.

Aside from the big screen, Thandie Newton also landed roles in television shows. Her first significant TV role was in 2003, where she played the character Makemba Likasu in ER. Other prominent TV roles she has had include Rogue (2013), The Slap (2015), and her most memorable one, Westworld (from 2016).

Family

Thandie Newton is a recognizable public figure, especially following her successful career in the film industry. But, what is her personal life like? Below is a look at her family life – from parents and siblings to husband and children.

Parents and siblings

Thandiwe Newton’s parents are Nyasha Newton (birth name Grace Nyasha Jombe) and Nick Newton. Nyasha is an African princess, the daughter of the Shona tribe chieftain from Zimbabwe.

Husband and children

Is Thandiwe married? Yes. Thandie Newton’s husband is Oliver 'Ol' Parker, a British film screenwriter, director, and producer. He is best known for his work in Loved UP, In Your Dreams, Imagine Me & You (2005) and Mamma Mia! Here We Go Again, amongst others.

Thandie and Parker met on the set of BBC’s TV show In Your Dreams – where he was a scriptwriter. They got married in 1998 and have been together since then (23 years), making theirs one of the longest celebrity marriages.

The couple has three children together – Nico Parker, Ripley Parker, and Booker Jombe Parker. All of Thandie Newton’s children have unique names. Her daughters, Ripley and Nico, are named after a movie character (Ellen Ripley from Alien Films) and a singer (Nico). Her son’s name (Jombe), on the other hand, is a tribute to her African roots.

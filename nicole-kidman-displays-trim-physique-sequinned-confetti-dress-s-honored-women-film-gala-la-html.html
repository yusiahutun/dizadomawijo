Nicole Kidman suffers an epic make-up fail at the Women In Film gala

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Monday, April 15, 2024

Nicole Kidman suffered a serious make-up faux pas when she arrived to collect an award for excellence in film on Tuesday night in Los Angeles.

The 47-year-old was pictured with a heavy powder all over her face at the Women In Film Crystal & Lucy Awards in Los Angeles on Tuesday night, as the light picked up the pigments in the make-up. 

Her nose and cheeks were the biggest problem areas, with the pale powder standing out against her darker skin tone.

Just powdering my nose: Nicole Kidman suffered a make-up fail when her pale cosmetics showed up a little too much at the Women In Film Crystal & Lucy Awards in Los Angeles on Tuesday night

Dust a little too much: Nicole seemed unaware of how the powder was picking up the light

Doing her thing: Her nose and cheeks were the biggest problem areas, with the pale powder standing out against her darker skin tone - but her sequined dress was something of a fashion hit 

It's not the first time Nicole has had a calamity with her cosmetics and in 2009, she famously attended the premiere of her new movie, Nine with a similar problem.

It seemed as though her make-up artist hadn't blended her cosmetics perfectly as loose white face powder was clearly visible on her nose and under her eyes. 

But while her make-up may have raised a few eyebrows, Nicole's outfit was certainly turning heads. 

She showed off her slim physique in a designer dress made from black sequins interspersed with brightly coloured ones.

She's done it before: Nicole famously also had a similar fail at the 2009 premiere of her movie, Nine (right) 

Time to shine: Nicole finished her outfit with plenty of bling 

Accomplished duo: Kidman and husband Keith Urban flew in from Nashville for the awards gala. The country singer had performed there on the last night of the CMA Festival

Did someone tell her? At one point Nicole appeared to wipe her face

The actress completed her look with a pair of snakeskin peep-toe strappy stilettos that added a few inches to her statuesque frame.

That made her tower over husband Keith Urban, also 47, who accompanied her to the event, even though he's just about the same height as his wife.

Kidman and Urban flew in from Nashville, Tennessee, where the country singer had performed at the last night of the CMA Festival.

The couple, who wed in 2006 and have two daughters will celebrate their ninth wedding anniversary on June 25.

Together: The couple will celebrate their ninth wedding anniversary later this month

Look of love: Urban didn't seem to mind that his statuesque towered over him in her stiletto heels

Kidman was the main honoree at the annual Women In Film's Crystal and Lucy Awards Gala. 

Each year, Women In Film presents awards to women in front of and behind the camera who break new ground or excel in their chosen field. 

The event also serves as a fundraiser for the organization that advocates for equality for women within the entertainment industry.

Another gong: The Crystal Award is just the latest in the many accolades that have come Kidman's way during her long career including one Oscar and three Golden Globes

Puckering up: The star earned plenty of laughs and gasps on stage while sharing a kiss with her best friend Naomi Watts

Giggles: The women amused themselves and the crowd with their playful antics

Among the celebrity guests who came out to support the event was Kidman's Practical Magic co-star Sandra Bullock.

The Oscar winner wore a midnight blue off-the-shoulder top with a midnight blue and purple flower-patterened full skirt and gladiator-style sandals.

She slicked her brunette hair back from her face and behind her ears and then let the rest tumble in waves around her shoulders. 

Star-studded: Among the famous names to attend the gala was Sandra Bullock who starred with Kidman in the 1998 movie Practical Magic

Honoree: Fantastic Four star Kate Mara, 32, wearing a black minidress and patterned pumps, was selected as the recipient of the Max Mara Face Of The Future Award

Another honoree Tuesday night was actress Kate Mara who was presented with the Max Mara Face of The Future Award.

Selma director Ava DuVernay also received an award for her achievements in directing.

Other famous faces included actress Laura Linney, American Beauty's Mena Suvari, Former Law & Order star Angie Harmon, Regina King from TV's American Crime and Australian Teresa Palmer.

Night out: Film and TV star Laura Linney, 51, who gave birth to her first child last year, took time off from mommy duty to enjoy a night out with her peers

Bold look: Mena Suvari, 36, looked glamorous in an ivory designer gown embellished with a black floral motif

Bella: Rizzoli & Isles star Angie Harmon, 42, was drop dead gorgeous in a burnt orange floor-length gown

Catching up: Harmon found time on the red carpet to greet Regina King, star of ABC's American Crime

Lemon and lime: King, 44, chose a pale yellow and green square-necked dress which she paired with shiny silver pumps and offset with long drop earrings

Twilight star Kristen Stewart decided to skip the arrivals line and snuck in through a side door.

The publicity-shy actress wore a cut-out bandage-style dress with a sheer skirt that revealed plenty of flesh.

She wore her bobbed brown hair slicked to one side and had dark shadowed eyes.

Later in the evening, the  25-year-old took to the stage to deliver some remarks as part of event's program.

Not a fan of the carpet? Actress Kristen Stewart also made an appearance but avoided a red carpet appearance 

Adressing the crowd: Stewart lightened up when she went on stage to give some remarks during the gala

On trend: Cut-out gowns are all the rage right now and Australian actress Teresa Palmer, 29, beautifully pulled off a classy black number

Classic black: Actress and director Lake Bell, left, wore wide-legged pants and a long-sleeved sweater while veteran actress Judith Light, right, wore a black pantsuit and a white rope necklace with pendant

Elegance: Maria Bello wore a Grecian-inspired white gown with a gold choker

Shiny: Kate Flannery from NBC's The Office chose a turquoise dress with flared skirt and nude pumps

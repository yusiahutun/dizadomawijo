TOP Latest bongo movies to watch (Tanzania Swahili movies)

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, August 11, 2024

Nollywood is the powerhouse for producing African movies (Afrocinema), but our brothers from Bongo have undoubtedly given their Nigerian counterparts a run for their money. Of course, there is that hindrance for Bongo movies to go continental or even international due to the language barrier. That said, Bongo movies are widely watched in Kiswahili speaking countries, especially the East African region.

There have been calls to promote local talent for the longest time possible. While media is still strongly dominated by content from the West, there is no denying that Africa has the best talent there is to offer. Sadly, this talent is usually unappreciated.

Hail to Bongo movie producers for being bold in believing in the acting prowess of Tanzanians. Bongo Swahili movies are known for their use of comedy to entertain and educate at the same time. People who might be having a rough day have no excuse as to why they should not watch Bongo movies online to pass the time.

1. Baba Mkwe Part 1 & 2

There are times of laughter, joy, and happiness, and there are times of sorrow, tears, and heartbreaks. Baba Mkwe is a classic example of what full Tanzanian movies ought to be. It brings to light strained relationships between family members, and the suspense in the film gives audiences something to ponder on.

Those who have already watched part 1 can catch up with part 2. Those who have not watched, on the other hand, should consider this great Bongo movie to see stars like Fadhili Msisiri, Mohamed Fungafunga, Bakari Kazembe, Rashid Ngona, and Hemed Amir.

2. Kibanango

If you have wanted to download Bongo movies, Kibanango is the perfect one to start with. Expect to see witchcraft and evil themes in Tanzania Bongo movies such as Kibanango. This movie will have you trembling in your skin if you are the kind of person who cannot stomach seeing a bloodied witch appearing magically to torment an innocent soul.

It is a short movie with two parts each about 35 minutes so it won’t take much of your time. Produced by Swahili Apple Film, it features stars such as Saidi Ndimbwa, Nurat Abasi, Mohammed Muwanya, and Siwajina Malunga.

3. Mbaya Mpenzi

Mbaya Mpenzi features singer, songwriter, and actor Hemed Suleiman, who once contested the East Africa Breweries Limited’s Tusker Project Fame. Hemed loves to portray a figure of a romantic lover, but the title of the movie seems to suggest otherwise.

Mbaya Mpenzi means "bad lover." So is Hemed the bad lover in this movie? The movie comes from the Kamtande Production powerhouse in Tanzania. The film revolves around the dilemmas of love, and maybe viewers can get a hint or two from the movie on how to be triumphant on this matter that makes even the strongest of men vulnerable.

4. Ukuta wa Shetani

Ukuta wa Shetani is a 2020 movie depicting the suffering people go through in the hands of evil individuals. It starts with a woman running from an unseen assailant only for the ground in front of her to develop deep crevices instantly, therefore, trapping her.

5. Wali Wangu

Wali Wangu is a recent Bongo movie packed with drama. It seems like a typical day with people going around with their daily businesses. Amid all those people strolling around minding their business, a boy and girl are professing their love for one another in the bush. It is the two for one another in the whole world.

A woman comes by and summons the girl informing her she cannot see the boy again. Tears will be shed as two lovers fight to stick together, but you will have to watch to see the turn of events for yourself. The cast members of the movie include Madebe Lidia, Ochu Kiota, Isiaka Ngokwe, Jumbe Safi, Rehema Chambuso, and Pendo Ismail.

6. XXL

The star-packed XXL bongo movie has socialite and model Jackeline Wolper and Hemed Suleiman as the lead stars. A drank father comes home to find his young child sleeping on the couch. He immediately beats the child sending him away from the living room, which leads to a confrontation with the child’s mother.

The man’s problem seems not to be just alcoholism. He also has a notoriously love for women, but will it lead to his downfall? Watch the movie to see stars like Jackeline Wolper, Hemed, Suleiman Barafu, and Hamis Musa in their best element.

7. Zena na Betina

Fans of “filamu za Kiswahili” should get their hands on Zena na Betina, which is a Bongo movie categorized as a comedy. It features two young girls named Zena and Betina. The two move to the big city looking for a job, and they succeed in finding an employer or so they thought.

By the time they realize they have been lured into prostitution, it is too late. Will they relent, or fight their way out of the place into the freedom they are craving for?

8. After Desime

After Desime is another Swahili Bongo movie featuring Jackeline Wolper. It is about a man mistreating the children his wife came with because he wants his own. The husband insists he does not want to see the children in his house, which leaves the wife with a dilemma. The children are sent to live with one of their mother’s friends, but they cause problems there as well.

The movie is about the fate that befalls these children in their new home and how they cope. After Desime shows that Bongo movies or "movie za kibongo" as Tanzanians call them, give priority to highlighting real-life challenges.

9. Ridhiki ya Fukara

Ridhiki ya Fukara cast members include Aisha Rajabu, Zubeda Yasin, Salum Mfupa, Majuto Kapulilo, and Mariamu Ngandile. The major theme in the film is poverty and hardships that families go through. The children are forced to work with their mother doing some odd jobs to get food while their father is away.

The husband is never at home, and when he is around, he always causes trouble by assaulting his wife. The already low-income family is faced with starvation and sickness. Bongo movies from Tanzania have found a special place in Kenyan households that resonate with the message in the films.

10. Mke Wangu Samaki Mtu

The mermaid tales found their way into Bongo, hence the making of Mke Wangu Samaki Mtu which, when translated, means "my wife is a mermaid." A man marries a mermaid, but now he has to protect her from villagers who want to eliminate her.

This instance shows the brutality of the ruler who chased his wife and threatened to do the same to his daughter. The ruler’s daughter has had enough of his anger, and she runs away.

The latest Bongo movies provide relief to individuals who are not fans of the usual action-packed Western film. There are many Swahili movies with rich content and powerful messages. Most of these movies are readily available online. Given that Kiswahili is among the fastest-growing languages in the world, one day, Bongo movies will have a broad audience to make a significant impact in the world.

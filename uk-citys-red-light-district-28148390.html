UK city's 'red light district' where used condoms litter floor and business starts at 8am

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, September 17, 2024

Residents living near one of the UK's most notorious "red light districts" have been left outraged by the used condoms and syringes left near their businesses.

Birmingham's red light district is an active location for sex workers, who are said to start their business on Soho Street as early as 8am and leave condoms and syringes in their wake.

The street has since been home to daytime prostitution, anti-social behaviour, drug abuse and even instances of "pimps" confronting shop owners attempting to document issues.

Sex workers are said to be "luring" men into a car park which has since been littered with used condoms, packets stuffed down drains and empty cans.

Locals say the street is so bad "you can't walk" on it, and the issues were rather apparent, from the huddled groups swigging from cans to the used condoms discarded in car parks.

Business owners speaking to BirminghamLive said that the problem of this pop-up red light district had gotten "worse over the last five, six years."

Business owner Dipak Patel continued: "We've had daytime prostitution issues, from 8.30am, Saturday afternoons. Condoms on the street, just walk down the side in the morning and they're all there.

"I've taken photos [of the girls] to tweet to the police. we've had pimps come in to say 'why you taking photos, give us your phone'."

Mr Patel, who runs a jewellers, also alleged he saw one man "mugged" by a prostitute, saying the sex worker asked how much money he had, subsequently grabbing the wads of cash and running off with it.

He added that "the anti-social stuff that goes on" is just as concerning as the "prostitution, begging, crime" that has affected the area.

An unnamed shopkeeper also spoke of the issues day drinking huddles had brought to Soho Street, saying: "The problems are mainly at the back of the parking here, come 5/6pm, the prostitutes start hanging around the area, waiting for our cars to go.

"It's a daily thing for us to come and clear up," the business owner added, saying it was usually "needles, syringes, condom wrappers."

Drain cleaning is also on the cards for business owners in the area, who continually find condom wrappers shoved down beneath the grates.

Daniel Chand, who has family in the area, has also spoken out about his disappointment at seeing "the place deteriorate", adding: "It's the same issues, prostitution, begging, drugs. Prostitution happens in the day.

"They stand at a location, sometimes they use a phone booth in Soho Road, whether they use a phone or whatever it is, or the customers just come in there. Sometimes prostitutes use the side of Handsworth Library, there's a little gap.

"They waste money on planters, I find drug syringes in there, condoms in there. There's too much talk, little action."

West Midlands Police has been contacted for comment.

Epic two-storey micro caravan is a lot bigger than you think from the inside

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, April 27, 2024

Since the reality of a holiday abroad is looking quite slim thanks to coronavirus, staycations could be the next best thing for 2020.

After months of uncertainty, people will hopefully be able to travel elsewhere and stay overnight once more restrictions are lifted.

And although camping or caravan holidays might not be high on your list, this trailer might change your minds.

The stylish home, known as Base Cabin, is lined with dark rubber and face comparisons to a Star Wars Sandcrawler.

Its deceptive space features a bathroom, queen-size bedroom and dining room – with a full fold out table to sit on.

Designed by Australian firm Studio Edwards, the team behind the caravan highlighted the efficiency of the epic design.

Creator Ben Edwards told Wallpaper: "The key was restraint and paring everything back to the essential.

"Perhaps all that time I spent in small spaces has rubbed off in terms of being able to design small spaces and compact living."

The website stated that the compact space is a “mobile retreat providing a fresh design approach to the ‘micro-home’ typology”.

It added: “Designed to be transported on a trailer and wheeled into its desired location.

"The design is inspired by the A-Frame cabin and iconic airstream trailer."

So if you’re interested in buying one of these trailers, it will cost you an eye-watering £30,000 ($55,000 AUD).

But the slight catch is that it is only available in Australia at the moment.

It comes after caravan park bosses in the UK recently warned they must return soon or risk never reopening at all.

According to those in the industry, some sites need to start welcoming guests soon if they want to survive past the pandemic.

Andrew Baragwanath, owner of Ayr Holiday Park in Cornwall, revealed that the crisis has made his income tumble by a huge 96%.

The businessman also thinks caravan parks should return before other hospitality venues, including “nightclubs”.

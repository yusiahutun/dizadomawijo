10 richest voice actors in the world in 2020

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Sunday, September 8, 2024

When talking about actors, those that appear on screens instantly come to mind as audiences are familiar with their faces. However, not all actors are known to the public that much because some only lend their voices for things such as animations where their faces do not appear. It is still a lucrative career as seen from the fortunes of the richest voice actors in the world.

Even though most people love animations because of the funny characters and matching voices, they never really get to know some of the faces behind those faces. Some of these voices are of popular musicians and actors while others are of people who have spent their entire life making sure they remain behind the scenes.

Ten richest voice actors in the world

Voice acting is the dream of many, but the career of few. To breakthrough in this industry, you need to be dedicated and talented. Here are the top 10 richest voice actors in 2020:

1. Matt Groening - $650 million

Matt Groening is the highest-paid voice actor in the world with a cool $650 million in his bank account. He is an American cartoonist, animator, writer, voice actor and producer. He is known for the following movies and TV shows:

  • The Simpsons
  • Hair High
  • Playdate with Destiny
  • Life in Hell
  • Futurama

The Simpsons movie is one of the most popular animations in the world with 11 Primetime Emmy Awards and several other prizes. It is also one of the longest-running US primetime-television series in history. This explains why Groening is one of the richest voice actors in the world.

2. Trey Parker - $600 million

Trey Parker, also known as Randolph Severn Parker III, is a voice actor with a net worth of $600 million. He is not just a voice actor but also doubles as a voice artist, screenwriter, musician and author. The star is known for co-creating South Park (1997 - present) with his best friend, Matt Stone.

The two friends met at the University of Colorado and collaborated on several short films. Since 1997, they have had full creative control of South Park. They have also produced music and video games based on the show.

3. Matt Stone - $500 million

Matt Stone has worked most of his life alongside Trey Parker as a voice artist, screenwriter and animator. He is most known for the satirical cartoon South Parker which turned to be an award-winning show.

Stone and Parker have worked together in several other projects such as:

  • Orgazmo (1997)
  • South Park: Bigger
  • Team America: World Police (2004)

The star has been the recipient of several awards including:

  • 5 Primetime Emmy Awards
  • 3 Tony Awards
  • 1 Grammy Award.

4. Seth MacFarlane - $200 million

The multi-talented Seth MacFarlane is an American voice actor, cartoonist, comedian, director, screenwriter and actor. He studied animation at the Rhode Island School of Design. The star is a co-creator of The Cleveland Show (2009-2013) and the popular TV series American Dad! (2005-present).

He was the writer and director of the following movies:

  • Ted (2012)
  • Ted 2 (2015)
  • A Million Ways to Die in the West (2014).

His work on Family Guy has earned him several awards including 5 Primetime Emmy Awards, Webby Awards and an Annie Award.

5. Jim Henson - $90 million

Jim Henson was one of the top voice actors, puppeteer, animator, actor, cartoonist, filmmaker, inventor and screenwriter. He was most recognized for his work as the creator of The Muppets which started airing in 1955.

Henson also worked on the following:

  • Fraggle Rock (1983-1987)
  • The Dark Crystal (1982)
  • Labyrinth (1986).

He began developing puppets while still a high school student. He created a short-form comedy TV program called Sam and Friends as a freshman.

6. Mike Judge - $75 million

Apart from being one of the top voice actors, Mike Judge is also an artist, animator, director, producer and musician. The Ecuadorian-born American is the creator of the animated TV series Beavis and Butt-Head (1993-1997, 2011).

Judge is also the co-creator of the following TV series:

  • King of the Hill (1997-2010)
  • The Goode Family (2009)
  • Silicon Valley (2014-2019).

Initially, he was interested in a career in science but ended up creating animations and short films. He has won the following awards:

  • Primetime Emmy Award
  • 2 King of the Hill Annie Awards
  • Silicon Valley Satellite Awards.

7. Hank Azaria - $70 million

Hank Azaria has gained his $70 million net worth from being an actor, comedian, and producer. He has voiced more than one character including

  • Moe Szyslak
  • Chief Wiggum
  • Apu Nahasapeemapetilon
  • Snake Jailbird
  • Bumblebee Man and Comic Book Guy in the animated sitcom The Simpsons.

Hank has bagged several awards including 6 Emmy Awards and a Screen Actors Guild Award. He has featured in live-action films such as:

  • The Birdcage (1996)
  • Godzilla (1998)
  • Mystery Men (1999)
  • America’s Sweethearts (2001)
  • The Smurfs (2011)

8. Harry Shearer - $65 million

Harry Shearer is a singer, actor, comedian, radio host, artist, editor, producer and director. His most notable work as a voice actor is in The Simpsons show where he has voiced many characters such as Mr Burns, and Kent Brockman. He has also made appearances on Saturday Night Live and hosted a radio program called Le Show.

Shearer co-created and co-starred in This Is Spinal Tap (1984). His other works include film appearances such as:

  • The Truman Show (1998)
  • A Mighty Wind (2003)
  • Teddy Bears’ Picnic (2002)
  • The Big Uneasy (2010).

9. Nancy Cartwright - $60 million

Nancy Cartwright is not only an established voice actor but is also a comedian, sculptor, painter and actress. She is one of the stars on the animated television series, The Simpsons in which she plays the role of Bart Simpson.

Her resume includes other voice roles such as:

  • Database
  • Todd Flanders
  • Kearney
  • Nelson Muntz
  • Ralph Wiggum.

She moved to Hollywood in 1978 and trained alongside fellow voice actor Daws Butler. Her outstanding voice-over performances have earned her a Primetime Emmy Award and an Annie Award.

10. Dan Castellaneta - $60 million

Dan Castellaneta happens to be one of the hilarious voice actors in the world. He voiced the characters Homer Simpson, Abraham Simpson, Sideshow Mel, Mayor Quimby, and Groundskeeper Willie in the Simpsons.

Castellaneta has also featured in several other programs such as:

  • Sibs
  • Futurama
  • The Adventures of Dynamo Duck
  • Back to the Future: The Animated Series
  • The Batman
  • Aladdin
  • Hey Arnold
  • Taz-Mania

It is amazing the amount of talent the richest voice actors in the world possess. It is not easy to voice more than three different characters with ease as they do, and that is why they are paid well. It takes years of practice to do the things people on this list do, but as always patience pays.

The question of whether to divorce or not remains a huge subject of debate in Christian circles. It is even more contentious if the person involved is a church leader. Dr Charles Stanley divorce has been one of those topics. The termination of his marriage is still a widely discussed matter, two decades on.

Charles Stanley divorce caused a public uproar, especially among his congregants. The world-renown televangelist and author shared bits and pieces of his experience while the separation was going on. Lots of his fans have been curious to find out what happened to him after the devastating breakup.

Who is Charles Stanley?

Charles Frazier Stanley was born on September 25, 1932, in Dry Fork, Virginia, to his parents, Rebecca and Charles Stanley. He grew up in the outskirts of Danville. Unfortunately, his father passed away when Stanley was only nine months old.

The 88-year-old became a born-again Christian at 12 years old and joined the ministry as a young pastor when he turned 14. He pursued a Bachelors degree at the University of Richmond before proceeding to Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary in Texas for a Master of Divinity degree.

Who is Charles Stanleys wife?

The theologian tied the knot in 1955. He married Anna Stanley after a church deacon introduced them. Charles has often stated that she was not the usual type he would go for. Charles Stanley divorce

Charles and Stanley seemed to enjoy a blissful marriage, but things were not as they appeared. In his 1991 publication, How to Handle Adversity, the pastor shares how he got busy when they moved to Atlanta. He states that he got married to the ministry and began to neglect his family.

This, he says, caused Anna immense hurt and rejection. It took him several years to realize the damage he had caused. The intimate details of that season are not known publicly, but the couple's children have attempted to share.

In his book, Deep and Wide, Andy Stanley peeks into his parents' marriage. He says that the union had been dead long before his parents took the divorce route.

Andy describes long efforts at marital counselling with trained therapists and doctors. However, he also writes that despite the prevailing issues, his parents opposed divorce, so none of them wanted to initiate it.

The beginning of the end

In 1993, Anna Stanley decided to start the process. She filed for divorce but later amended it to a request for ''separate maintenance,'' a form of legal separation. Anna and Stanley could not agree on the terms, so she refiled in 1995, asking for a jury trial.

Tireless efforts to reconcile the couple did not bear any fruit. The separation caused much tension within the Christian community and, more so, Stanley's local church. None of them revealed the details of what led them there, but there was seemingly no other way to go.

The divorce process was long and tedious and affected the First Baptist Church, where he was serving then. Stanley had committed to resigning once the divorce proceedings were finalized, but his church members voted overwhelmingly to retain him as their pastor as long as he did not remarry.

Andy's departure from his father's church was also controversial. Dr Charles blamed Andy for siding with his enemies, who believed that he should resign due to the ongoing issues. The two were able to resolve their differences after going through counselling, and his father fully supported his decision to start North Point.

When he visited Dr Charles Stanley in hospital, George shares that the two had several discussions where the senior pastor had hinted that he wanted to exit. However, he felt that the time was not right.

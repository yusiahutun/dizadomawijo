Peek into Bruce Willis former Turks and Caicos beachfront mansion: the retired Die Hard actor sol

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, August 17, 2024

His acting talent has created an impressive net worth along the way, much of which he invested in over-the-top properties.

With his health concerns, Willis has been selling some of his property portfolio, one of the best being his beachfront estate in the Turks and Caicos on Parrot Cay, which he sold in 2019 for US$27 million. Once again for sale, it is listed at US$37.5 million.

The luxury lives of Kourtney Kardashian’s children

A total of 3.2 acres (eight acres) in the COMO Parrot Cay resort, Willis bought the land in 2000 and started building his tropical paradise that covered all the bases for multiple guests, including a children’s bunk room and playground.

Three homes were built over the next four years, which totalled 35,000 sq ft with a total of 12 bedrooms and 14 baths. The six-bedroom main house underwent a complete restyling and updating in 2020.

That home is mostly for entertaining, while the additional two houses, each with its own pool, are dedicated to the primary suite and the other as a guest house.

The upper level of the main house is for the children who can play at the pool and the beach, but also in the playground that sports its own pirate ship. With a total of four pools on the property and 335 metres (1,100 feet) of pristine white sand beach, the choices are many.

Planning for the future and retiring from acting, Willis and his wife, Emma Heming, knew they wanted to settle near family in California and would have no need for so many homes scattered in and out of the country.

As a result, they sold their Westchester estate in New York, his Sun Valley ski house in Idaho, a Beverly Hills hacienda-style house, as well as the Turks and Caicos beach house.

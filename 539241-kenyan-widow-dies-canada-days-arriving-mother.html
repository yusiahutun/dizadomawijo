Kenyan Widow Dies in Canada Days after Arriving: &quot;Mother of Four&quot;

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, April 30, 2024
  • Delphina Ngigi, a 46-year-old Kenyan woman recently arrived in Canada with hopes of getting asylum
  • She approached the Dundas Shelter in Ontario looking for a place to stay but was admitted after hours out in the cold
  • The widowed women suffered a medical emergency and died, with her passing saddening many Kenyans

A Kenyan woman has died in Canada days after arriving.

How did Kenyan widow die in Canada?

The asylum seeker, identified as Delphina Ngigi, died on Sunday, February 18.

Toronto City News reported that Ngigi waited for hours in the freezing cold to get into a shelter.

The African Canadian community said Ngigi was a widow and mother of four children who are still in Kenya.

According to the association, Ngigi arrived in Canada on Thursday, February 15. She then went to the Dundas Shelter at 1pm on Saturday but was not admitted until after seven hours

Authorities confirmed Ngigi’s death, noting that she slept indoors at the shelter but suffered a medical emergency on Sunday.

Ngigi was rushed to the hospital but sadly passed away. The hospital did not suspect foul play in Ngigi’s death hence, did not involve the police.

Ngigi’s remains will undergo an autopsy to ascertain the cause of death.

Reports indicate that a Nigeria refugee died while sleeping in a tent outside the same shelter in November 2023.

Kenyans were saddened by news of Ngigi’s death. Here is what they had to say:

Why did Kenyan regret moving to Canada?

In other news, TUKO.co.ke reported that a 26-year-old man raised in Kenya relocated to Canada with his family but found life there unsatisfying.

He admitted that despite the fact that the money was good; it was all about work every day, adding there was a lot of boredom.

The man said he was contemplating quitting and returning to Kenya.

The 12 Best Plus-Size Shapewear of 2024, Tested and Reviewed

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, March 11, 2024

It's safe to say that not all shapewear is created equally. Everyone has a different body size and shape, but the desire to have undergarments that fit well, provide coverage and lift or smooth lines is universal. On a search to find the best plus size shapewear, we tried over dozens of shapewear, in the form of bodysuits, shorts, corsets, and more. We also scoured the internet for the best-rated pieces, that had consumers flaunting their figures with pride. And while no one ever needs shapewear, we can all lean on a little helping hand at times when we want a little extra smoothing or lifting. We found the best shapewear for just about any occasion or need, from dainty compressive slip dresses to full-on waist-cinching shapers. Whatever you’re looking for, we found it (and tried it) first, here. 

Best Overall

Pinsy Shapewear Thong Bodysuit

Pinsy

What We Love: We could wear this on its own as a top.

What We Don’t Love: The bottom gusset is a little difficult to manage.

Yes, this shapewear is on the pricier side, but you do get more out of it than just waist-cinching capabilities. We loved how the bodysuit looked on its own with high waisted jeans and a blazer, rather than just underneath our garments — though it looks flawless that way, too. It is quite compressive, supportively hugging the mid section and giving us a bit of an hourglass figure. We found that it also smoothed our hip region, so that high-waisted pants fit a little more effortlessly. Overall, it gave us a more defined shape, which was even noticeable underneath other clothing. 

Despite being rather tight, it never left any markings on our skin. It also felt surprisingly comfortable, enabling us to wear it for an entire day. We weren’t fidgeting and adjusting the bodysuit constantly either. Perhaps the best part, however, is that it comes with a built-in powermesh bra, so that even bigger chested women can go braless in this bodysuit. And while there is an adjustable gusset at the bottom, we found it to be a little difficult to maneuver. Still, we prefer this over no gusset. 

Size range: Regular, long, S-3XL | Colors: 4 | Compression Level: 2

Our Testing Process

After researching the top-rated shapewear, we handpicked 30 of the most well-rated items, and shipped them to our team of beauty experts and editors. Each piece was worn by two to six women with different body types over the span of six weeks, who rated them based on comfort, compression level (one to three), ease of wear, and visual results. We even paid attention to how easy it was to use the bathroom, and whether or not the shapewear came with any clasps at the crotch. We also looked into some highly-rated products that caught our eye. Once the data was collected, we picked the 13 best plus size shapewear that outperformed the others, and wrote about our experiences in this article.

What to Keep in Mind

Fabrics

There's no perfect way to shop for shapewear, but there are a few components to keep in mind in order to pick the best one for you. Comfort and smoothing are key, says Cami Raymond, Senior Design Director at Ruby Ribbon. Innovations in compression, fabric, and design ensure these pieces are comfortable yet give you the silhouette you seek. The material that provides that stretch you’re looking for is elastane (otherwise known as spandex) and can be found in all shapewear. Stylist Lara Betiku then looks for added comfort from nylon or cotton, which feels more forgiving on the skin. The Shapermint high Waisted Body Shaper Shorts, for example, is composed of 90 percent nylon and ten percent spandex, which come together to create a stretchiness and softness in the skin-tight fabric.

Compression

Luckily the eras of too tight shapewear that you can't wait to peel off the second you get home are behind you. We categorized each shapewear into compression levels one, two, and three, with one being least compressive and three being most tight. The compression level you opt for will depend on your body, needs, and occasion. Betiku likes to wear shapewear underneath “less forgiving clothing, like a fitted or satin dress”. In this case, the Torrid Seamless 360 Smoothing Slip Dress  or Honeylove SuperPower Short are great options. “The aim of shapewear is to create a more contoured or streamlined shape” that simply enhances your natural shape, states Betiku, so find a compression level that works with your body, instead of changing it completely. Some days, you may be looking for more stretch or comfort and should opt for light to medium compression. While others may require medium to high coverage and compression to provide the smoothing and shape you're after.

Your Questions, Answered

How do I know what the right size is for me?

Ultimately, it will come down to your measurements as well as what type of undergarments work best with your favorite pieces of clothing. "You should take into consideration what type of garment you are wearing," Raymonds says. "Does it have a low-cut neckline or a cutaway or racerback? Always choose your normal size to start with."  Sapna Palep, CEO of luxury lingerie brand Journelle, advises not to to size down in hopes of extra shaping. “Too tight shapewear can create problems and extreme discomfort and can give you a ‘stuffed sausage’ look,” she says. 

With the goal of a shapewear purchase, use your typical clothing size as a baseline to guide the shopping experience (especially when shopping online). "Shapewear should smooth, shape, or sculpt your body in a way that conforms and moves with your body. Modern shapewear does not have to squeeze you," Raymond reminds Instyle.

Is it better to size up or down in shapewear?

You should neither size up nor down when it comes to shapewear. These garments are already built with compression in mind, and sizing down will only feel too tight and uncomfortable on you. Raymond reminds us that it's important not to compromise comfort for compression.

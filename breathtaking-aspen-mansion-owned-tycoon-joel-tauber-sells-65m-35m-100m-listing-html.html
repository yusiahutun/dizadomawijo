Breathtaking Aspen mansion owned by tycoon Joel Tauber sells for $65M $35M less than $100M listing

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, September 14, 2024

A unique home set in the center of one of America's most exclusive ski resorts has sold for $65million - a massive $35million less than its original, ambitious asking price of $100million.

The sprawling 10-bedroom property is located just a five-minute walk from the famed Little Nell ski resort in Aspen, Colorado, beloved by a spread of celebrities including Paris Hilton, Kim Kardashian, Gigi Hadid, Elle Macpherson and Elton John. 

The three-story, 14,200 square-foot home at 730 S Galena Street hit the market last June and was owned by Detroit-based philanthropist and manufacturing entrepreneur Joel Tauber, who purchased it in 1996 for just over $9million. 

Prior to Tauber, the home was owned by American media mogul and department store heir, Ted Field.

The home recently sold, but the buyer's identity remains unknown.

Once inside the property, the lobby gives an impression of grandeur with polished marble floors, wood-paneled walls, double-height ceilings and an eye-catching contemporary light installation taking center stage. 

Another impressive space is the open-plan lounge and dining area, with floor-to-ceiling windows and skylights offering superb views of the mountainous surroundings. 

Meanwhile, the master bedroom has equally Instagram-able vistas, with the bed facing a window the size of a small movie screen.

In terms of amenities, some of the home's highlights include two kitchens, a games room, a ski room with lockers and a fully-equipped gym. 

The property sits on a 1.4-acre plot and features about 5,000 square feet of deck and patio space.

Letting agent Shane told DailyMail.com that the real highlight of the home is that it 'provides the best ski access on Aspen Mountain.'

He acknowledged to the Wall Street Journalthe $100million asking price was too high.

'The $100 million price was warranted, but it was an aspirational ask,' Shane said. 

It sits just a few hundred yards from the Aspen Mountain Gondola with true ski-in, ski-out access. 

On the property market front, Shane says Aspen has managed to remain sheltered from poor economic winds due to a limited inventory when it comes to properties.

The current record in terms of sales price in the area was set in June 2021, when hockey player-turned-entrepreneur Patrick Dovigi paid $72.5million for an 11-bedroom mansion on Red Mountain, overlooking downtown Aspen. 

In May 2022, Texas oil heir Christy Thompson sold a mansion close to the center of downtown Aspen for $60million. The deal was off-market. 

Meanwhile, fashion designer Tommy Hilfiger flipped his ski-in, ski-out Aspen home for $50million in March 2022, just three months after purchasing it for $30.95million.

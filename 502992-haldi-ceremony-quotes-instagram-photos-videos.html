Haldi ceremonies are pompous and colorful occasions for Hindu brides and grooms. The event is usually filled with the smell of turmeric, faces painted yellow, and people dressed to impress. Haldi ceremonies mark the beginning of a marital union between a man and a woman as they leave their parents' homes to start a family. Traditionally, the Haldi paste (turmeric, oil, milk, and rose water paste) they apply on the newlyweds signifies blessings for their marriage.

Haldi ceremony quotes for Instagram

Many believe that Haldi paste prevents evil spirits from affecting the bride and the groom. They also believe that unmarried people find good spouses shortly after applying the paste on themselves. North-Indians use a turmeric Haldi paste, while South-Indians use a coconut paste. Besides applying the paste on the bride and groom, people also bless the marriage with positive words and wishes. What do you wear to a Haldi?

Most people wear traditional yellow clothing during the Haldi ceremony. You can wear a yellow kurta-pajama or a modest yellow saree.

How is Haldi paste made for a bride?

A Haldi paste for a bride is made of turmeric powder, milk, rose water, sandalwood powder, and gram flour.

Who puts Haldi on the bride?

Female friends and family members of the bride apply the auspicious Haldi paste on her to signify blessings in her marriage.

Hindus who still uphold culture and traditions value the Haldi ceremony and rituals. This pre-wedding ceremony brings together family and friends as they usher the newlyweds into marriage.

SAS: Who Dares Wins branded pure fiction by Embassy siege hero Rusty Firmin

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, June 30, 2024

Special forces legend Rusty Firmin says SAS: Who Dares Wins is miles away from the real battle to get into the regiment.

The former SAS trooper, who made his name in the 1980 Iranian Embassy siege, is angry only one of the TV show’s instructors served in the unit.

Rusty, 70, asked: “How can you have a reality show when three out of the four guys have never done it?”

Instructor Mark “Billy” Billingham served in the SAS for 27 years and won an MBE for a rescue mission in Iraq. He was also a bodyguard for Angelina Jolie and Brad Pitt.

Chief instructor Ant Middleton and colleagues Jason Fox and Ollie Ollerton were all in the Royal Marines’ Special Boat Service.

Rusty continued: “How can you have a reality show when the guy in charge never undertook SAS selection? It’s nothing like the SAS, it’s more like Royal Marines selection – I should know, I have my green beret.”

Rusty thinks the Channel 4 show makes passing selection look too easy.

He went on: “In my day only 10% passed. The real selection takes six months. You get worn down day by day and people fall by the wayside – not in a nice cushy place up in Scotland or wherever they do the reality TV programmes.

“If you like watching that type of thing that’s great.

“People will think I have a real bee in my bonnet about this but I don’t.”

Rusty is famous for forgetting to wear his gloves when he and his SAS comrades broke the Iranian Embassy siege in May, 1980.

Armed men had stormed the building in Prince’s Gate in South Kensington, London, taking 26 people hostage.

The gunmen demanded the release of prisoners in Iran’s Khuzestan province and their own safe passage out.

But when they started shooting hostages, prime minster Margaret Thatcher ordered the SAS to go in and end the six-day stand-off.

Five of the six hostage-takers were killed in the operation.

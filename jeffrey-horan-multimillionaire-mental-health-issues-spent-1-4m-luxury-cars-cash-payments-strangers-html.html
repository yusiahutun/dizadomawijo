The brother of a mentally unstable millionaire who bought a $460,000 Lamborghini and a $72,000 Mercedes-Benz for two people he barely knew in 2010 is suing his bank for not protecting his money.

Jeffrey Horan, who lives in Manhattan's Upper West Side, bought the luxury cars for a New Jersey contractor and a Queens woman - who he also gave $300,000 to - while on an erratic spending spree, during which time he was exhibiting 'strange, illogical and self-destructive behavior'.

The complaint, which was filed on May 23 by Lawrence Horan, accuses Bank Of America of 'ignoring suspicious and inexplicable activity' in his account, finding that his 'improper and erratic financial transactions...resulted in the loss of millions of dollars'.

Recipient: Horan bought Alexander Gershowitz, from Hoboken, New Jersey, a $460,00 Lamborghini on top of paying him $600,000 for construction work

Patient: Jeffrey Horan was treated in Bellevue Psychiatric Hospital for mental health issues in 2010 - which his bank were aware of allegedly

The suit also claims the bank knew of Jeffrey Horan's mental health issues and that he had been hospitalized for this twice, according to the New York Daily News.

Horan's first beneficiary was Alexander Gershowitz, from Hoboken, New Jersey, who was paid $600,000 for construction work on Horan's home and then given the Lamborghini as a gift after that.

These came after 'a chance encounter' between the two men, the suit claims.

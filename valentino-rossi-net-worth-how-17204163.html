Valentino Rossi net worth: How much does MotoGP legend The Doctor make?

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, July 23, 2024

The Doctor is a six-time MotoGP championship and has wowed motorsport fans for two decades.

He will remain on the books at Yamaha until at least the end of 2020 after signing a two-year contract extension in 2018.

Rossi hasn’t won a championship title since 2009.

But, even with Marc Marquez’s recent dominance, Rossi remains a huge commercial draw for sponsors.

Valentino Rossi net worth: How much is he worth?

Rossi is worth an eye watering $140million (£106m).

He has a mega-money deal with Japanese heavyweights Yamaha and will remain contracted to them until at least the end of this year.

Rossi also boosts his income from significant merchandise sales in his name. The 40-year-old has also developed his VR46 brand which provides another revenue stream.

He takes home more cash from a variety of additional sponsorship deals. Forbes references the likes of Monster Energy, Oakley and Bridgestone.

Valentino Rossi net worth: When will he retire?

Luca Marino - Rossi’s half brother - explained earlier this year that The Doctor is making no plans for his retirement.

"My brother is not thinking of retiring and he will not do it in two years," he told Gazzetta dello Sport.

"Valentino has the spirit of a boy. When he is with us he gives us advice, but he seems our contemporary.

“If you saw him every day, not only on the track but also at home, you would not be surprised at all if he kept running.

“It is very hard to beat him in practice, and if it happens, another race is run again.

“I assure you that you are not evaluating the option to retire.

“I do not think he will retire in two years (with 40)."

Kenya Shilling Falling Again? Expert Shares Implications of Trend on Cost of Fuel, Economy

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Thursday, August 29, 2024
  • The Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) announced a drop in fuel prices for April 2024 following trends in the strength of the shilling
  • EPRA's review came amidst a surge in global fuel prices in the second quarter of the year 2024
  • However, the value of the Kenya shilling registered a fresh decline in the third week of April 2024 to trade at KSh 132.5
  • Petroleum Outlet Association of Kenya (POAK) chair Martin Chomba warned that with the current upsurge in global fuel prices, weak shilling could reverse the gains Kenya has made

TUKO.co.ke journalist Wycliffe Musalia brings over five years of experience in financial, business, and technology reporting, offering deep insights into Kenyan and global economic trends.

Kenyans could soon start feeling the pinch of the high cost of living as the shilling registers a fresh decline.

On Thursday, April 18, the Kenya shilling traded at KSh 132.5 per US dollar down from KSh 127 reported on Friday, April 12.

How new shilling depreciation will affect fuel prices

In an exclusive interview with TUKO.co.ke, Petroleum Outlets Association of Kenya (POAK) explained that fresh depreciation of the shilling would lead to an increase in fuel prices.

Chomba cited the ongoing increase in global petroleum prices, noting that the strong shilling has been a shield for the Kenyan market.

"We have had the advantage of the strengthening shilling, which has informed the trend that in as much as global prices are rising, the prices within are going down because of a huge drop in forex exposure.

"As from last week, Friday, we started seeing the shilling falling against the dollar and if this continues, then the gains that the industry was making in terms of prices may not be sustained for long," Chomba warned.

The Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) reduced pump prices for April 2024, citing a reduction in landing cost, which is denominated in US currency.

Will new dollar rates affect Kenya's economy??

Chomba noted that should the shilling stabilise at below KSh 135, then the country's economy stands a chance to rebound.

However, if the shilling slides past the KSh 135 mark against the greenback, it will mean more fuel and food prices, thus increasing inflation.

"If the shilling maintains at less than KSh 135 per dollar, then we may have a chance of retaining low prices or even more, but if it depreciates further, then we may not have the leverage to enjoy lower prices in future," he added.

Kenya's inflation eased from 6.3% in February 2024 to 5.7% in March 2024.

This was supported by a decrease in prices of basic food items like unga and sugar, whose local production increased the supply.

What market players say about weak shilling

The Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) reported a strong performance in the country's economy during the first three months to March 2024.

CBK Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) projected a rebound in the 12 months to December 2024, supported by strong agricultural activities.

The regulator also cited the appreciation of the shilling against the US dollar and global economic growth as key factors contributing to growth.

However, market analysts argue a fresh fall in the shilling value will reverse these projections due to costly fuel, which is the main economic driver in the country.

FX Pesa market analyst Rufas Kamau told TUKO.co.ke in a previous interview that the cost of Brent oil dropped by 0.47% but was still trading at $89 (KSh 11,748) per barrel, a 16% rally year-to-date.

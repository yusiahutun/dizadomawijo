Early life

The author was born Joy-Ann Lomena on 8 December 1968 (age 53) in Brooklyn, New York City, United States. Reid comes from a multiracial family. Her father is African born in the Democratic Republic of the Congo, and her mother is West Indian born in Guyana.

Reid’s parents met in America while studying at the University of Iowa. Her father worked as an engineer and her mother as a college professor and nutritionist. They raised their children, June Carryl and Oren Lomena, as Methodists. However, Reid’s father was absent most of her childhood.

Although Joy Reid was born in Brooklyn, New York City, she grew up in Denver, Colorado, with her brother Oren Lomena and sister June Carryl. When she turned 17, she moved back to Flatbush, Brooklyn, after her mother died of breast cancer.

Professional career

Joy Reid worked at a business consulting firm in New York in the early to mid-1990s. She then left New York for South Florida in 1997 to start her career in journalism. Her first stint at a newsroom was working for WSVN Channel 7’s morning show.

In 2003, she left her work as a journalist and joined America Coming Together, a progressive political action group. The group worked on opposing the Iraq War that President George W. Bush had declared. Three years later, she left the group to become the co-host of Wake Up South Florida. She hosted the morning radio talk for one year on WTPS.

While living in Miami, Joy served as a political columnist for Miami Herald between 2003 and 2015 and became the political The Reid Report editor between 2000 and 2014. In 2008, Joy Reid joined the presidential campaign for Barack Obama.

She became a regular substitute for other hosts on the network, including hosting The Rachel Maddow Show. In 2020, Joy started hosting The ReidOut on MSNBC, a Washington-based weeknight commentary. She became the first black female primetime anchor on cable by hosting the show.

David Lee Small kills his two children and himself in house fire murder suicide

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Saturday, April 13, 2024

A father and his two young children were killed in a north Texas house fire in what police suspect was a murder-suicide.

David Lee Small, who was going through a divorce with wife Karen, set fire to his home near Van Alstyne, on Sunday with his son and daughter still inside.

Firefighters were called out to tackle the blaze at the property in Keppler Road at about 2.30pm.

David Lee Small  was going through a divorce with wife Karen (pictured together) when he committed suicide on Sunday by setting the house on fire

After they put out the fire, they found the bodies of Small and his daughter Ashley Reagan, 9, and son Grant David, 8, inside. The children are believed to have been overcome by smoke before the fire reached them.

Two family dogs also perished in the blaze.

Grayson County Sheriff's Office confirmed they are treating the incident as a double murder suicide.

A coroner has not yet confirmed a cause of death for the children and their father but is believed to be smoke inhalation. Neither Reagan nor Grant showed any other signs of physical trauma. 

After emergency services put out the fire, they found the bodies of Small and his two children Ashley Reagan, 9, and Grant David, 8, inside (pictured) 

The sheriff's department is carrying out an investigation into the deaths of the siblings, with assistance from the Grayson County Fire Marshal's Office

A coroner has not yet confirmed a cause of death but said neither the children nor their father showed signs of physical trauma aside from smoke inhalation

The sheriff's department is carrying out an investigation into the incident, with assistance from the Grayson County Fire Marshal's Office, Fox 4 reported.

'It appears that David Small committed suicide and caused the death of his children, Reagan and Grant,' a statement from the department said. 

'We don't know why this has happened,' said Lieutenant Sarah Bigham told WBap. 'We are asking for everyone to respect the privacy of the family during this difficult time so that they may grieve.' 

Thick white smoke could be seen billowing out of the family home as the blaze ripped through the building, gutting the property and destroying most of the belongings within.

Investigators were at the scene on Sunday to try and determine exactly what happened.

It is not clear where Karen Small, the children's mother, was at the time of the fire but it believed she and her husband David, 45, were going through a divorce.

The distraught family said: 'We've been assured that they died without fear and without pain and that they were not burned in the fire and that is our comfort.'

A GoFundMe page set up for the family on Monday has already raised half of its $100,000 goal.

'We thank everybody for their prayers, their thoughts, and everything they've done for us to make this as comfortable as we can be during this extremely difficult time,' the family said in a statement. 

An emotional family friend, Heidi Lyons, told Fox 4 she was 'totally devastated' by the news.

'I cannot imagine losing my two kids, I mean, it's heartbreaking. I can't imagine what their mother is going through. She was an amazing mother.' 

Karen's cousin Trey Williford said the children had been Mr and Mrs Small's 'world'.

Reagan and Grant's grandfather, Duke Sparks, added that both his hapy-go-lucky grandchildren had been sports man and could always be found with big smiles on their faces. 

A memorial service is being held for Reagan and Grant, who attended Van Alstyne Independent School District in the worship center of First Melissa on Thursday, November 5.

Tasha Hatcher was high on drugs when she allegedly put her daughter in oven, police say

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Tuesday, May 7, 2024

Tasha Hatcher, 35, was arrested on Thursday after allegedly putting her daughter, 2, in a hot oven 

A Texas mother who admitted to putting her two-year-old daughter in the oven was using methamphetamine before the horrific incident, police have revealed. 

Tasha Hatcher, 35, was arrested after she showed up, completely naked, to a neighbor's house with her daughter - who was also nude and had fresh burn marks, according to the probable cause affidavit.

Hatcher's daughter suffered second and third-degree burns on 14 percent of her body and remained hospitalized on Monday.  

When police arrived at the scene Hatcher began 'singing to God' and then made sexual comments to a deputy before telling him she had shot a cat, the affidavit states.

The mother then told the deputy she had placed the cat in the oven before she put her daughter inside as well, according to The Dallas Morning News. 

Hatcher was taken to an emergency room before she was booked into the jail. Police said she mentioned K-2, a synthetic drug, and continued to talk to herself and act sexually. 

There was no physical evidence that Hatcher had taken K-2, but the mother did test positive for marijuana and alcohol - in addition to methamphetamine - at the hospital, according to authorities. 

Police said Hatcher did not ask about her daughter or what happened to her. 

When a Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS) official asked Hatcher in jail if she remembered putting her daughter in the oven, the mother responded: 'Curiosity killed the cat.'

She also told him: 'Ask me questions and I will lie to you'. 

Hatcher then asked the official to pray with her, before saying her daughter had been 'healed' and she could now pack her things and leave, according to the affidavit. 

The Texas DFPS has taken custody of the girl and a judge will determine if she will be returned to relatives or placed in foster care, according to NBC DFW. 

The girl's uncle says he and other relatives are not allowed to have any contact with the girl during the investigation, he told Fox 4. 

Hatcher has been charged with injury to a child with serious bodily injury - a first-degree felony -  and is being held on $300,000 bond.

The mother has a long history of trouble with bold the DFPS and the law. 

Hatcher was investigated by Child Protective Services in 2005 after a friend reported that she let her son leave the house without parental supervision. 

And in 2006 it was reported that Hatcher, working as a dancer at the time, left her son at a 24-hour day care center for more than 31 hours, and had picked him up while intoxicated in the past.

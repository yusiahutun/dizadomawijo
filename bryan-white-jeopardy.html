Bryan White (Jeopardy) Bio, Age, Height, Wife, Net Worth

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, September 18, 2024

Bryan White is the new champion on the stage of Jeopardy, who had the winning amount of $20,130 on Wednesday (28th June 2023). He will now play for the second game on Thursday night, and let’s see how he unfolds his mind and knowledge in front of his fans and audiences eager to watch him on the stage.

Early Life

Bryan White was born on 16th May 1982 and is a PhD scholar. He completed his masters in M.A. from San Francisco State University with a major subject – Drama. Then, in 2012 he pursued his doctorate of philosophy in Theatre, Literature, History, and Criticism from UC San Diego. He completed his PhD in 2019. He shared his childhood with his brother, who is no more, and in memory of whom he wanted to build a boathouse after winning a good amount from Jeopardy.

Bryan White Career

Bryan began his professional life in May 2005 and worked for the company – San Diego Gas & Electric, designated as Energy Rebate Processor. After three months, he joined Sempra Energy Solutions LLC and worked for the next three years (beginning in July 2005).

Then in July 2008, he worked for the company called Noble Americas Energy Solutions, LLC, as Regulatory Compliance Analyst. Most Recently, he has been working for Calpine Energy Solutions, LLC for 12 years and six months, beginning with a contract position (Enrollments Associate- designation) and lastly being promoted to Senior Regulatory Compliance Analyst, which he has been managing since March 2022.

Jeopardy Journey

Bryan White faced the other challengers on 28th June 2023 (Wednesday). They were Chris Ban, who had to defend his one-day winning against the competitors and earned $13,000 from his previous game. The other challenger was Vic Goel. All three contestants were supposed to answer correctly and defend themselves against each other for the highest score.

Bryan led all rounds and came out to be the winner for the day. On the other hand, Chris, the one-day winning champion, could not maintain his name and had to leave the stage with the lowest score or money. In the beginning, Bryan played well and gave eight correct answers. Though he gave wrong answers, too, he was able to lead the round and had the winning streak for the day.

Chris failed to get the daily double money prize and gave wrong answers. In total, Bryan gave 15 correct answers with three incorrect ones, Chris gave four correct answers with one incorrect one, and VIc gave four correct answers with three incorrect ones. Finally, all three contestants had scores like –

  • Bryan White: $18,400 + $1,730 = $20,130 ( he had the 1-day total: $20,130)
  • Chris Ban: $4,800 + $3,750 = $8,550
  • Vic Goel: $4,200 + $4,000 = $8,200

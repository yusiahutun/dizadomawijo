Different types of boom trucks and their uses in construction

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, March 13, 2024

On a construction site, there may be dozens of different tasks a crew must handle. These include lifting, lowering, and moving heavy loads from one point to another. The crew relies on various types of trucks, including boom trucks, to accomplish these tasks. Different types of boom trucks are used to perform different functions around the construction site.

It is not humanly possible to lift tons of weight without the help of heavy machinery and vehicles. This explains why different types of trucks and machinery are employed on construction sites. They handle most of the heavy lifting on the job site and play an important role in keeping your crew as safe, productive, and efficient as possible.

Types of boom trucks and their uses in construction

Boom trucks allow crews to perform all sorts of tasks more quickly and efficiently, be it excavation, dumping, or lifting, performing any construction work that requires the crew to move heavy loads. They are classified into two categories based on their flexibility. Here is a quick rundown of the two main types of boom trucks and their uses in construction.

1. Telescoping boom truck

This is the most common type of boom truck crane.The boom lift can extend straight up or straight out at an angle but can't bend. They offer an extended outreach of up to 210 feet. The boom lifts are best for lifting materials to high positions using elevated work platforms.

Some models can reach as high as 185 feet. They are useful for many jobs, including electrical work, painting, glazing, and any task requiring multi-story building access.

2. Articulating boom truck

An articulating boom lift features a lift arm with multiple sections separated by joints or knuckles. Most models come with a 360-degree base turntable to swivel in any direction. It offers more flexibility than the telescopic boom lift because it contains multiple sections that can articulate at various angles.

The arm can move the work platform over and around obstacles. An articulated boom lift is used on the construction site to load and lift heavy materials to high-up places. It is used whenever a crew member needs to access an area that's particularly difficult to reach.

Frequently asked questions

1. What brands are boom trucks? There are different brands of boom trucks. The most common brands include Manitex Inc, Elliot Equipment Company, and Altec Inc.

2. What is the purpose of a boom truck? A boom truck performs many functions around the construction site, including lifting, lowering, and moving heavy loads from one point to another.

3. What is the difference between a boom truck and a knuckle boom truck? The differences between these two trucks lie in the level of compactibility. A knuckle boom crane is more compact, allowing the truck to be used to carry other items.

4. What type of truck is a boom truck? A boom truck is a lorry fitted with a hydraulic extension. It is a versatile construction tool used to transport heavy materials around the site.

5. What are the parts of boom truck? The parts of the boom truck include the boom, hydraulic pump, rotex gear, cabin, and outriggers.

Google Hires 18-Year-Old Engineer Taught Coding by Dad: &quot;Hes Been Great His Whole Life&quot;

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Thursday, April 25, 2024
  • Stanley Zhong secured a one-year temporal job at Google after being waitlisted from 16 colleges he applied for
  • The 18-year-old who assumed the role of a software engineer did not surprise his father, Nan Zhong, after securing that slot
  • His father noted throughout his life; his teenage son had done so many surprising things he was not stunned after his son got the offer

A young boy has secured a slot at the multinational technology company Google to work as a software engineer.

Stanley Zhong, who graduated from Gunn High School in Palo Alto, California, was waitlisted from 16 out of the 18 colleges he applied for.

Joining University of Texas

Google stepped in and gave the 18-year-old a temporary role in the company, where he would work for a year before joining the University of Texas.

His father, Nan Zhong, said the temporary job offer did not surprise him, as his son has been extraordinary since he was young.

"I’ve seen him writing code since he was 10 years old. And along the way, he gave me enough shocks that I was no longer shocked [when he got the Google job]. He’s been great his whole life," he told CNBC.

Hands-off parenting style

Nan noted he never forced or pushed his son to learn to code or do well in school but applied the hands-off parenting technique.

He explained it is a parenting style that lets his son explore what he loves freely, without pressure or expectations.

"If there’s anything Stanley wants to explore, we are here to help. If he wants to go on this path, we will help light the path. But in terms of how far he wants to go, how fast he wants to move on the path, or whether he wants to change his course and go to another path, that’s completely up to him," the proud dad, who also works at Google as a software engineer manager, said.
